Find examples of our previous completed jobs to help you select the level of paint finish you would like for your army. Each level of paint will include a brief description of what it includes so you containermake the best choice for you.

Basic

This is the base option, if you want to just get your army ready for tournaments or just to play games at your local club, this is the option for you.This is also our cheapest option so it is perfect for armies with large horde elements. Despite being our most basic option all work is carried out to a high standard.

Stages of painting includes:

  • A high level of basecoating.
  • Multiple levels of wash applied.
  • A high standard of drybrushing to pick out all the edges.

Table Top

This is the most popular option, it come with a few extra steps to ensure each model looks life like. We reccomend this option for people who want to play games while having an amazing looking army.

Stages of painting includes:

  • A high level of basecoating with exact colours each model needs.
  • Pre highlighting applied with an airbrush(this helps give an apperance of light shining down on the models).
  • A high level of targeted washing.
  • Edge highlights applied using a drybrush and the standard edge higlighting method.
  • Weathering will be applied where applicable
  • Small details like helmet lenses, eyes and teeth etc are all painted.
  • Transfers are applied if needed.

Display

This option is for people who want an army that looks amazing either on the table top or in a display cabinet. With this option only our best artists are put on the job, with decades of experience they know how to make your models look the best they can be with techniques like wet blending and targeted highlight airbrushing.

Stages of painting includes:

  • A high level of basecoating with exact colours each model needs.
  • Pre highlighting applied with an airbrush(this helps give an apperance of light shining down on the models).
  • A high level of targeted washing.
  • Edge highlights applied using a drybrush and the standard edge higlighting method.
  • Small details like helmet lenses, eyes and teeth etc are all painted.
  • Weathering will be applied where applicable
  • Display level will see the most usage of more time consuming techniques including airbrushed glows and effects, stencil usage and wet blending. The artist will combine a variety of effects and materials to ensure the model is pushed to a highly pleasing appearance suitable for primarily being used for display.
  • Transfers are applied if needed.
